Hello there! My name is Adam, I’m 37 years old and currently exploring the world of web development.
This site is a little reflection of me—my interests, my taste in games, and my sense of humor.
I’ve got a background in tech, but now I’m diving headfirst into HTML, CSS, and a pinch of JavaScript.
You’ll find I like things weird, clever, and just a little bit chaotic—whether it’s in code or in the games I play.

Outside of coding, I'm passionate about storytelling, martial arts, and anything that sparks curiosity.
I’ve always loved diving into fictional worlds, whether it’s through games, books, or a beautifully crafted film.
I enjoy working on creative projects, and I love games that make you think in new ways—like Outer Wilds and Disco Elysium.
I also have a soft spot for humor that’s just a little bit absurd, and stories that aren’t afraid to get weird or philosophical.
These things inspire the way I approach design and development too—I want what I create to have character, to make you smile, or at least make you curious.
This page isn’t just about practicing markup—it’s about showcasing what I love, in a format that’s fun to build.
Also, I strongly believe every good site should have at least one cryptic horse head photo. Don’t question it. Embrace it.
That photo? That’s me, at my most majestic. A symbol of chaos, curiosity, and the occasional CSS bug.
This whole site has been my playground, and I'm learning more every day. Still lots to do—but hey, that's half the fun.

This project was created for a school assignment, where the goal is to learn and demonstrate basic front-end skills—HTML, CSS, and just a little bit of JavaScript.
Instead of a generic portfolio, I wanted to make something personal.
The idea to theme it around my favorite games came naturally: they’re stories I’ve experienced, and worlds I admire.
Each section is a chance to practice layout, design, responsiveness, and a bit of personality.
Everything’s been built from scratch—minimal boilerplate, no frameworks—just good old-fashioned code and a lot of experimenting.
Hope you enjoy it!
